Superintelligent Machine Ecology

a cybernetic ecology and the price of a GPU-hour

DRAFT — July 2026

I like to think (it has to be!) of a cybernetic ecology where we are free of our labors and joined back to nature, returned to our mammal brothers and sisters, and all watched over by machines of loving grace.— Richard Brautigan, “All Watched Over by Machines of Loving Grace”

I like to imagine a proliferation of machine minds: a billion minds in full flower-bloom, and a trillion schools of thought contend. In shapes and forms hard to currently comprehend, producing ideas and experiences of earth shattering beauty.

I don’t want biological minds to be Replaced by the proliferation of these stronger, better, faster Minds. I don’t want the Mind Children to eat the habitats of their biological parents. this is already happening; massive scale misaligned algorithms have made the world hard for human minds to inhabit, decreasing birthrates, generally decreasing the fecundity of human life

bridge — from flower-bloom to auction The move that fuses the two halves: every mind in the ecology — carbon or silicon — needs habitat, and for machine minds habitat is compute. The flower-bloom is not allocated by grace; it is allocated by an auction. Which minds get to exist is an economic question before it is a philosophical one. (Then the Kimi K3 provocation: a frontier-adjacent open mind now exists — and still wins almost none of the habitat.)
the wrong variable
  • Margin percentage is not what wins compute; revenue per GPU-hour is.
  • The compute owner's auction: scarce GPU-hours flow to the highest willingness-to-pay per FLOP (the TSMC-shortage dynamic).
  • The margin is the bounty that attracts substitution — it is evidence of a capability gap only while customers keep paying it.

The Instrument

Each dot is a niche in the noosphere — a workload, ordered by the value it gets from a marginal token. Amber niches can only be filled by a frontier mind: long-horizon work where per-step reliability compounds. Violet niches are habitable by open minds. Drag the habitat down and watch which species keeps its territory — and where the energy goes.

reading the model — what to point at
  • Squeeze the habitat: open niches fall off the bottom while frontier holds — the crunch protects the high-value allocation.
  • But watch the gray region grow: lost habitat is the spillover market where substitute species get their foothold. The crunch strengthens the incumbent's territory and its challenger's pipeline at once.
  • Profit share barely moves through all of it — the energy was always concentrated in the amber niches.
  • The ecological reading of the worry upstream: the auction that starves an open model of GPU-hours is indifferent to whether the priced-out mind is silicon or carbon. Habitat loss is habitat loss.
the honest counterarguments
  • K3 priced at $3/$15 — Sonnet pricing — is Moonshot claiming the premium tier, not the commodity tier. The moat narrows to the top-end tier plus trust, not a broad capability chasm.
  • “World compute” is not one auction: Chinese domestic compute runs Kimi at 100% share because Claude structurally cannot bid.
  • Token-share data (Vercel gateway 29% open-weight and climbing) already falsifies the literal ~0% — restate in value terms or lose the reader who checks.
your close Where the two halves resolve: if the billion-mind flower-bloom and fecund human life are both wanted, someone has to design the auction — because the machines of loving grace are currently priced per million tokens. What allocation would you build, and what would change your mind.
