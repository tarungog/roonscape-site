The Highest-Value FLOP

who gets the GPU-hour when there aren’t enough GPU-hours

DRAFT — July 2026

your opening — the claim The provocation that started this: Kimi K3 is genuinely good, yet you expect ~0% of world compute to run it instead of Claude. The strong version of the claim, sharpened: open models will win tokens; frontier closed models will win the gross-profit pool — and a compute crunch concentrates the profit pool faster than it erodes it.
setup — the wrong variable
  • Margin percentage is not what wins compute; revenue per GPU-hour is.
  • The compute owner's auction: scarce GPU-hours flow to the highest willingness-to-pay per FLOP (the TSMC-shortage dynamic).
  • The margin is the bounty that attracts substitution — it is evidence of a capability gap only while customers keep paying it.

The Instrument

Each dot is a workload, ordered by the value it gets from a marginal token. Amber workloads require a frontier model — long-horizon agents where per-step reliability compounds. Violet run fine on an open model. Drag the compute supply down and watch who keeps their GPU-hours — and where the profit goes.

reading the model — what to point at
  • Squeeze supply: open workloads fall off the bottom while frontier holds — the crunch protects the high-value allocation.
  • But watch the gray region grow: that unserved demand is the spillover market where substitutes get their foothold. The crunch strengthens the incumbent's allocation and its challenger's pipeline at once.
  • Profit share barely moves through all of it — the pool was always concentrated in the amber dots.
the honest counterarguments
  • K3 priced at $3/$15 — Sonnet pricing — is Moonshot claiming the premium tier, not the commodity tier. The moat narrows to the top-end tier plus trust, not a broad capability chasm.
  • “World compute” is not one auction: Chinese domestic compute runs Kimi at 100% share because Claude structurally cannot bid.
  • Token-share data (Vercel gateway 29% open-weight and climbing) already falsifies the literal ~0% — restate in value terms or lose the reader who checks.
